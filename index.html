CSS Colours Cheatsheet

Primary Colours

  • In the Additive RGB Color Model primary colors are those colors when adding together produce white
  • Each color must be at its highest intensity. For this to happen we use the rgb function with the parameters as red, green and blue
  • Using the primary function in CSS is choosing an additive system. The function starts at rgb(0,0,0) which is black rgb(0,0,0). You increase the colors of each parameter up to 255 away from black or adding to black. When all colors are added to their highest intensity we get white rgb(0,0,0)

Secondary Colours

  • These are the colors you get when you combine primary colours
  • yellow: rgb(255, 255, 0)
  • cyan: rgb(0, 255, 255)
  • magenta: rgb(255, 0, 255)

Tertiary Colours

  • These are the colors you get when you combine primary colour with a nearby secondary colour
  • orange rgb(255, 127, 0) is made from high(full) intensity red and a decreased intensity of green. This is because orange is a combination of red and yellow and falls between these colours in the colour wheel.
  • spring green (cyan and green): rgb(0, 255, 127)
  • violet (blue and magenta I think): rgb(127, 0, 255)
  • chartreuse green(green and yellow): rgb(127, 255, 0)
  • azure (blue and cyan): rgb(0, 127, 255)
  • Rose(red and magenta): rgb(255, 0, 127)

Complementary Colours

  • Two colours that are on opposite sides of the colour wheel
  • If two complementary colours are combined they produce grey
  • When two complementary colours are placed side by side they provide string visual contrast and appear brighter

*** Notice that the red and cyan colors are very bright right next to each other. This contrast can be distracting if it's overused on a website, and can make text hard to read if it's placed on a complementary-colored background. It's better practice to choose one color as the dominant color, and use its complementary color as an accent to bring attention to certain content on the page. ***

We use complementary colours to attract the readers attention to certain parts of the webpage.
